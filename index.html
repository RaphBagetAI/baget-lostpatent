The Telharmonium (1897): The 200-ton Additive Synthesizer

The Telharmonium, built in 1897, was a massive electromechanical instrument that converted electrical energy directly into musical tones using huge alternators. It transmitted music over telephone lines long before electronic synthesis was invented. The deeply intricate gear ratios and multiple rotor systems allowed the Telharmonium to generate a surprisingly wide range of sounds for its size and era.

This invention represents a pinnacle of early 20th century electro-mechanical innovation but was ultimately lost due to its immense size and power requirements, which made it economically unfeasible. Detailed schematics reveal the complexity of the rotating electrical generator design, inspiring modern engineers interested in unusual mechanical synth designs.
